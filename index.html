Experimenting with NEAT for Stock Trend Prediction

As an intermediate AI Engineer, I'm always fascinated by exploring new approaches to existing problems. This project delved into the world of Neuroevolution of Augmenting Topologies (NEAT), a technique for evolving neural network architectures. My goal? To investigate its potential for predicting stock trends.

Technical Details

  • NEAT Library: The project utilized the NEAT library (link to documentation: https://lnkd.in/g3YP4CAA).
  • Dataset: Historical data for TSLA stock, including Open, Close, High, and Low prices.
  • Goal: Predict future trends (upward/downward) instead of absolute stock prices.
  • Network Input: The model achieved better results when fed with the previous day's OHLC (Open, High, Low, Close) data compared to using only the past 5 days' closing prices.

Why NEAT?

While established models like SARIMA exist for stock prediction, NEAT offers a unique advantage. Its ability to evolve neural network architectures through generations allows the network to automatically discover optimal configurations for the specific task. This holds the potential to surpass hand-crafted architectures.

Diving into NeuroEvolution of Augmented Topologies (NEAT)

Hey everyone! Today I'm taking a deep dive into a fascinating paper by Kenneth O. Stanley and Risto Miikkulainen: Evolving Neural Networks through Augmenting Topologies (NEAT). This groundbreaking work explores how to evolve neural networks not just for better weights, but for better structures too!

Imagine this: You're training a robot arm to grab a cup. Using traditional methods, you tweak its "muscle" settings (weights) endlessly. With NEAT, you also evolve the arm's anatomy itself, maybe growing extra fingers or adjusting their length! This leads to much faster and more creative solutions.

How NEAT Works

Historical Markings

Think of these as birthmarks for each part of the network. They keep track of how each node and connection came to be, preventing "reinventing the wheel" and ensuring valuable structures survive.

Speciation

Like in nature, NEAT encourages diversity by grouping similar networks into "species." This prevents one dominant design from wiping out all others, allowing exploration of different approaches.

Incremental Growth

Just like a baby, NEAT networks start simple and gradually add complexity, ensuring efficient evolution and avoiding getting overwhelmed.

The Big Deal

NEAT opens up a whole new world of possibilities for AI:

  • Robotics: Design robots that adapt to their environment, walk on any terrain, or even learn new skills like climbing.
  • Game AI: Train agents that strategize, learn from mistakes, and even create their own tactics in games like Pong.
  • Creative AI: Generate art, music, and even write stories by evolving neural networks that explore new avenues of expression.

Advancements and Extensions

But NEAT isn't alone! Researchers are constantly building on its foundations:

  • HyperNEAT: Optimizes not just network topology, but also the evolution process itself!
  • Compositional Pattern Producing NEAT (CPPN): Creates modular neural networks, easier to understand and adapt for specific tasks.
